Option A — Add Product (matches your sidebar)

Option B — Add a use case

If your dashboard nudges you toward use cases instead, look for Use cases in the sidebar and add "Manage everything on your Page." That use case bundles in pages_manage_posts (plus related Page permissions), which then surfaces them on the Permissions and Features page.
A heads-up on what you'll likely run into: Meta has tightened this over the past couple of years, and the classic "Pages API" product isn't always offered as a standalone add-on anymore on newer apps — many now have to go through the use-case path. So if Add Product doesn't show a Pages option, go straight to the use-case route.

When both routes are missing like this, it almost always comes down to the app's type, which is fixed at creation. The products and use cases an app can access are determined by the type you picked when you created it, and Page management is gated to specific types (Business, or the newer "Other → Business" path). Your app was most likely created as a "Consumer" or similar type that exposes Facebook Login but not Pages.
Two things to check before deciding what to do:
1. Confirm the app type. Go to App settings → Basic and scroll down — the App type is listed there (often near the bottom, sometimes shown as "App type" or under the business/category info). If it says anything other than Business, that's your blocker.
2. Check whether a Business portfolio is connected. Also on App settings → Basic, there's a business/portfolio field. Page use cases sometimes stay hidden until a verified Business portfolio is linked. If it's empty, linking one might surface the use case — worth trying before the nuclear option.
The honest bottom line: app type generally can't be changed after creation. If your app is the wrong type, the clean fix is to create a new app and choose the Business type (or, in the current creation flow, select the "Manage everything on your Page" use case during setup). That makes pages_manage_posts available from the start. You'd then move your Facebook Login config over, which is quick since it's just a few settings.
